# Agent Onboarding Protocol

## Overview
This document defines a comprehensive step-by-step onboarding protocol for welcoming and integrating new employees into the organization. It serves as a single source of truth for HR personnel and new hires, ensuring a smooth transition and rapid productivity ramp-up.

---

## 1. Pre-Arrival Preparations

### 1.1 HR Actions (Completed Before Day 0)
- Send formal offer letter and employment contract.
- Collect signed contracts and required identification documents.
- Prepare employment paperwork (tax forms, emergency contacts, confidentiality agreement, code of conduct).
- Coordinate with IT for system access provisioning (email, project tools, code repositories, internal dashboards).
- Arrange workstation setup (hardware, monitors, peripherals, software licenses).
- Schedule orientation sessions and notify relevant team members.
- Prepare welcome pack (company handbook, swag, access cards).

### 1.2 New Hire Actions
- Complete and return all required paperwork before start date.
- Review company handbook and preliminary policies.
- Confirm personal details for payroll and benefits enrollment.

---

## 2. First-Day Agenda

### 2.1 Morning (09:00 – 12:00)
- **Welcome & Introduction** (HR + Team Lead)
  - Office tour and facility safety briefing.
  - Issuance of access cards, hardware, and welcome pack.
- **Paperwork Finalization**
  - Sign any remaining documents.
  - Benefits enrollment assistance.
- **System Access Activation**
  - IT walkthrough: email setup, VPN, project management tools, code repositories, collaboration platforms.

### 2.2 Midday (12:00 – 13:00)
- **Team Lunch** – Informal introduction to immediate team and key collaborators.

### 2.3 Afternoon (13:00 – 17:00)
- **Company Culture & Values Presentation** (HR or Culture Officer)
  - Mission, vision, core values, DEI policies.
- **Role-Specific Overview** (Direct Manager)
  - Job expectations, initial projects, reporting structure.
- **Mentor Assignment Briefing**
  - Introduction to assigned mentor, outline of mentorship program.
- **Setup Check & Q&A**
  - Ensure all tools and accounts are functional.

---

## 3. Training Schedule

### Week 1 – Foundation Training
- **Day 2–3**: Company systems training (internal wiki, ticketing, CI/CD pipelines, security protocols).
- **Day 4–5**: Role-specific technical training (frameworks, libraries, tools relevant to position).
- Assign initial learning resources and reading materials.

### Week 2 – Hands-On Practice
- Pair programming or shadowing sessions with mentor.
- Small tasks to apply learned concepts in a controlled setting.
- Begin attending team stand-ups and sprint planning.

### Week 3–4 – Integrated Work
- Take ownership of scoped tasks within team backlog.
- Participate in code reviews (giving and receiving feedback).
- Continue bi-weekly 1:1s with manager and mentor.

---

## 4. Mentorship Program Details

- **Duration**: Minimum of first 90 days, extendable based on role.
- **Pairing**: Each new hire is assigned a senior team member as mentor.
- **Meeting Cadence**:
  - Daily check-ins (first 2 weeks).
  - Twice weekly 1:1s (weeks 3–8).
  - Weekly 1:1s thereafter or as needed.
- **Mentor Responsibilities**:
  - Guide onboarding tasks and clarify processes.
  - Provide technical and cultural advice.
  - Monitor progress against 30/60/90-day goals.
- **New Hire Responsibilities**:
  - Come prepared with questions and blockers.
  - Actively apply feedback and reflect on learnings.

---

## 5. 30 / 60 / 90-Day Checkpoints

### 30-Day Checkpoint
- **Goal**: Complete foundational training, gain basic familiarity with team workflows.
- **Outcomes**:
  - All system accesses functional.
  - Understanding of team processes (stand-ups, reviews, deployments).
  - Completed at least one minor task or bug fix.
- **Review**: Manager + Mentor assess adaptation, address gaps.

### 60-Day Checkpoint
- **Goal**: Contribute meaningfully to team deliverables.
- **Outcomes**:
  - Ownership of scoped features or improvements.
  - Active participation in sprint ceremonies.
  - Demonstrated application of company coding standards.
- **Review**: Retrospective on achievements, refine goals.

### 90-Day Checkpoint
- **Goal**: Operate semi-independently within team norms.
- **Outcomes**:
  - Delivered at least one end-to-end feature or substantial improvement.
  - Established network within broader organization.
  - Positive peer feedback on collaboration and output.
- **Review**: Formal performance discussion, career path alignment.

---

## 6. Paperwork, System Access & Cultural Orientation

### Paperwork Checklist
- Employment contract & NDAs
- Tax withholding forms
- Emergency contact information
- Benefits enrollment forms
- Code of conduct acknowledgment

### System Access Provisioning
- Corporate email account
- Access to code repositories (GitHub/GitLab/Bitbucket)
- Project management tools (Jira, Trello, Asana)
- Collaboration platforms (Slack, Teams, Confluence)
- Build & deployment environments
- Internal dashboards & monitoring tools

### Team Introductions
- Immediate team members
- Cross-functional partners (Product, QA, DevOps)
- Key stakeholders and leadership

### Cultural Orientation Topics
- Company mission, vision, values
- Diversity, Equity & Inclusion initiatives
- Communication norms and meeting etiquette
- Feedback culture and growth mindset
- Social events and employee resource groups

---

## 7. Roles & Responsibilities

- **HR**: Orchestrate logistics, paperwork, benefits, culture sessions.
- **IT**: Provision and verify all system accesses.
- **Manager**: Define role expectations, oversee training plan, conduct check-ins.
- **Mentor**: Provide day-to-day guidance and support.
- **New Hire**: Engage actively, complete tasks, seek feedback.

---

## 8. Success Metrics
- Time to first commit/deployment
- Completion rate of onboarding tasks
- 30/60/90-day review scores
- Peer and mentor feedback ratings
- Retention rates post-onboarding

---

## 9. Continuous Improvement
- Quarterly review of onboarding feedback from new hires.
- Update protocol to address bottlenecks or gaps.
- Maintain template checklists for HR and managers.
